Explore the Epics: Ramayana and Mahabharata Trivia

The Ramayana is an ancient Indian epic depicting the journey of Prince Rama to rescue his wife Sita from the demon king Ravana. It illustrates themes of righteousness, duty, and the triumph of good over evil. Knowing it fosters cultural understanding and moral teachings.

Question 1

Who is the author of the Ramayana?

Answer: The Ramayana is traditionally attributed to the sage Valmiki.

Question 2

Who is Rama's wife?

Answer: Sita is Rama's wife.

Question 3

Who is Rama's loyal brother?

Answer: Lakshmana is Rama's loyal brother.

Question 4

Who is the demon king in the Ramayana?

Answer: Ravana is the demon king in the Ramayana

Question 5

What is the name of Ravana's kingdom?

Answer: Ravana's kingdom is Lanka

Question 6

What is the name of Rama's father?

Answer: King Dasharatha is Rama's father.

Question 7

What is the name of Rama's kingdom?

Answer: Ayodhya is the kingdom ruled by Rama.

Question 8

What is the name of the bridge built by the monkeys to reach Lanka?

Answer: The bridge built by the monkeys is called Rama Setu or Adam's Bridge.

Question 9

What is the name of Rama and Sita's twin sons?

Answer: Lava and Kusha are the twin sons of Rama and Sita.

Question 10

Who is Ravana's brother and a devout follower of Rama?

Answer: Vibhishana

Question 11

What is the name of Rama's bow?

Answer: Shiva Dhanush or Pinaka or Kodanda.

Question 12

Who is the father of Hanuman?

Answer: Vayu, the god of wind, is considered Hanuman's father.

Question 13

Who is the mother of Ravana's son Indrajit?

Answer: Mandodari is the mother of Indrajit.

Question 14

What is the name of the demoness who disguises herself as a golden deer?

Answer: Maricha is the demoness who transforms into a golden deer.

Question 15

Who cuts off Surpanakha's nose?

Answer: Lakshmana cuts off Surpanakha's nose.

Question 16

How many years did Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana spend in exile?

Answer: They spent fourteen years in exile.

Question 17

Who is the mother of Lord Rama?

Answer: Queen Kaushalya is the mother of Lord Rama.

Question 18

What is the name of the weapon Rama used to defeat Ravana?

Answer: Rama used the Brahmastra, an arrow given by Lord Brahma, to defeat Ravana.

Question 19

How many chapters (kandas) are there in the Ramayana?

Answer: The Ramayana is divided into seven chapters or kandas: Balakanda, Ayodhyakanda, Aranyakanda, Kishkindhakanda, Sundarakanda, Yuddhakanda, and Uttarakanda.

Question 20

What is the central theme of the Ramayana?

Answer: The central theme revolves around the victory of good over evil and the importance of dharma (righteousness).

The Mahabharata is an ancient Indian epic that tells the story of a great war fought between two rival families, the Pandavas and the Kauravas. It explores themes of duty, righteousness, and the consequences of actions. Alongside the battle, it contains teachings on ethics, philosophy, and spirituality. The epic is a cornerstone of Hindu mythology and culture.

Question 1

Who wrote the Mahabharata?

Answer: Maharishi Vyasa is traditionally credited with composing the Mahabharata

Question 2

What is the main theme of the Mahabharata?

Answer: The main theme revolves around the conflict between the Pandavas and the Kauravas, representing dharma (righteousness) against adharma (unrighteousness).

Question 3

Who were the Pandavas?

Answer: The Pandavas were the five sons of Pandu, a king of Hastinapura. They are Yudhishthira, Bhima, Arjuna, Nakula, and Sahadeva.

Question 4

Who were the Kauravas?

Answer: The Kauravas were the hundred sons of King Dhritarashtra, the blind king of Hastinapura. Duryodhana was their eldest brother and leader.

Question 5

Who was Draupadi?

Answer: Draupadi was the wife of the five Pandava brothers.

Question 6

Who was the charioteer of Arjuna?

Answer: Lord Krishna served as Arjuna's charioteer during the Kurukshetra War.

Question 7

Who was the teacher (guru) of both the Pandavas and the Kauravas?

Answer: Dronacharya was the teacher of both the Pandavas and the Kauravas.

Question 8

Who was the mother of Kauravas?

Answer: Gandhari was the mother of the Kauravas.

Question 9

Who was the mother of Pandavas?

Answer: Kunti was the mother of the Pandavas.

Question 10

Who is Krishna's sister in Mahabharata?

Answer: Subhadra is Krishna's sister, who later becomes the wife of Arjuna.

Question 11

What is the name of Lord Krishna's divine city?

Answer: Lord Krishna's divine city is named Dwarka.

Question 12

How many chapters (parvas) are there in the Mahabharata?

Answer: There are 18 parvas in the Mahabharata.

Question 13

What is the name of the celestial bow of Arjuna?

Answer: Arjuna's celestial bow is named Gandiva.

Question 14

What is the name of the kingdom ruled by the Pandavas?

Answer: Indraprastha is the kingdom ruled by the Pandavas.

Question 15

Who killed Bhishma in the Kurukshetra War?

Answer: Arjuna is responsible for the fall of Bhishma in the Kurukshetra War.

Question 16

Who is the mother of Dronacharya's son Ashwatthama?

Answer: Dronacharya's wife's name was Kripi. She is the mother of Ashwatthama.

Question 17

What was the name of the celestial weapon used by Arjuna to kill Karna?

Answer: Arjuna used the divine weapon called "Anjalika" or "Pashupatastra" to defeat Karna.

Question 18

Who was the son of Arjuna and Subhadra?

Answer: Abhimanyu was the son of Arjuna and Subhadra.

Question 19

What is the approximate date of the Mahabharata war?

Answer: The Mahabharata war is believed to have taken place around 3100 BCE.

Question 20

What is the significance of the Bhagavad Gita in the Mahabharata?

Answer: The Bhagavad Gita is a conversation between Arjuna and Krishna that takes place on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, where Krishna imparts spiritual wisdom and guidance to Arjuna.
